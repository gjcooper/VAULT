# Reunion Blurb

OK team - this is Heather Cooper A 68 year old woman from the Mid-north coast. We’re not exactly sure how she has conned her way into a short term stay here, but we think it might be something to do with the renovations she has organised for her own home. I understand she is here on a trial basis while her home is updated with the latest improvements for in home assisted care.

Heather is in good physical health. She has had a hip replacement like many of our other residents, and there are early signs that she may need another. However you wouldn’t know it to see her, it barely slows her down and she can move at a fair pace if needed.

In regards to mental health, Heather suffers from a condition called analysis paralysis. Symptoms include constant agonising over decisions such as picking the colour of her new kitchen cupboards. Along with the constant second guessing and switching there is also a healthy dose of decisional regret after the decision is made that is associated with the condition.

Unlike most residents here it is unclear whether Heather has actually retired from work. My understanding is she sold her irrigation supplies business, which she built from the ground up, to another person within the family. However it seems she is finding it a little difficult to let go and is a regular volunteer there to this day.

In terms of activities, a good book and some peace and quiet is usually enough to occupy her for quite some time. Or alternatively a glass (or three) of white wine and some family or friends to catch up with will keep her busy for hours as well.

However If the library runs out of books (or the cask of win runs out), the last resort is to ask for her help to organise an event. I know for sure some of our other staff have taken advantage of her penchant for organisation, taking some of the weight off their own shoulders. I must admit though that she does get immense satisfaction out of seeing everything go smoothly.
