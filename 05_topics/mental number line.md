The mental number line is the internal representation of numbers in peoples head when reasoning about relationships between numbers.

There are numerous effects observed in numeric reasoning tasks.

The mental number lines may be different in different contexts, for instance when reasoning about money, probabilities, ...


# Number lines effects

## Size Effect

The size effect is the finding that in judgements of two numbers to identify the larger, the size of the numbers has an effect on response times.

Specifically smaller numbers (1 vs 2) lead to faster judgements as opposed to larger numbers (8 vs 9)

## Distance Effect

The distance effect is the finding that in judgements of two numbers to identify the larger, the numeric distance between the numbers has an effect on response times.

Specifically larger distances (2 vs 8) lead to faster judgements as opposed to smaller distances (4 vs 6)

## Compatibility Effect

The compatibility effect is observed in multidigit numbers - there is a speed up in responses if all digits are greater than the digits in the comparison number.

That is 65 vs 43 is faster than 63 vs 45.

## Decade-crossing effect

Determining the midpoint between two numbers is slower and less accurate when the tens digits differ, ie bisecting 46-52 is harder than bisecting 33-39.
