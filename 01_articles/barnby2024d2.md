## My Thoughts

Some of [[Joseph Barnby]]'s work on paranoia and modelling of others [[harmful intent]] in a modified [[Dictator Game]]. The responses here are a straight slider where participants selected both perceived harmful intent and self-interest of their partner in the game based off of their partners choices to either split the money or take all of the money. There were three partner types, one split money 50:50 every trial, another always took all the money and the third partner type had a 50% chance of doing each of the above two actions.

The participants were drawn from the general population (all male) with multiple test days - each test day they were randomly assigned to either [[placebo]], [[haloperidol]] or [[L-DOPA]]. Behavioural results (see and referenced earlier report) showed haloperidol influenced behaviour, with participants on haloperidol reporting lower ratings of harmful intent of partners.
