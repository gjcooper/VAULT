## My Thoughts

A call to action echoing [[Jeffrey Rouder]] and [[Julia Haaf]] that studying behaviour using traditional quantitative methods, especially those relying on the standard aggregate measures that are typical can obscure important individual differences. Rather investigating the qualitative patterns in individual choices is important both for the case where aggregate measures can obscure the true breadth of behaviour at an individual level, as well as providing an avenue for theory development.

[[Isabel Thielmann]] provides a useful example from the personality and ethical decision making literature that highlights that rather than people all following the same relationship between incentives and lying, different classes of people have different interactions between incentives and willingness to lie.
