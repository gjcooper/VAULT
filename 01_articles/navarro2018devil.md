## My Thoughts

A good commentary piece on the tension between selecting between models from a purely statistical perspective. A range of different methods are covered here, but focus is given to [[Bayes Factor]] approaches and to [[cross validation]] in particular.

However the main thrust of the article is that the [[model selection]] from a statistical perspective is in [[Danielle J Navarro]]'s opinion unsatisfying. What Danielle would like to see more focus and thought on is the selection between different models from a scientific judgement perspective. That is, which model not only explains the data generating process, but has generalisability to new contexts in which the data generation process may be systematically different.

That is generalisation not just in the statistical sense to new unseen data from the same process, but the generalisation you might want to extrapolate from a simplified experimental data to real-world applications.
