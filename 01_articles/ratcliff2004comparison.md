## My Thoughts

An early comparison of various [[evidence accumulation models]], including the [[diffusion decision model|Wiener diffusion]] model of [[Roger Ratcliff]], and the [[Orstein-Uhlenbeck model]] (A diffusion model variant with decaying drift rate). Also considered were absolute stopping rule models such as an [[accumulator model]] and a [[poisson counter model]].

As this was a relatively old paper other more recent comparisons may be better placed as this was before the introduction of [[Scott D. Brown]] and [[Andrew Heathcote]]'s [[Linear Ballistic Accumulator]]. Additionally all fitting was done on the basis of group mean data rather than individual participant data, leading into the debates of group averages not representing any individual.

Also unsurprisingly the [[diffusion decision model|Wiener diffusion]] model fared surprisingly well in the comparisons, however my knowledge of the comparison models are lacking for a judgement on this basis.
#### How do they know this?

Comparisons of model fits to four experiments (group means of RT quantiles). They also compared fits qualitatively to simulated data from each other model (simulate from one model and fit with another) to investigate model mimicry.

#### What would I need to believe to accept this argument?

I would like to see updated comparisons with more recent additions to the model groups, as well as fits to individual data.

#### What questions does this raise that aren’t addressed?

There was a reasonable amount of model mimicry. This can be reduced with appropriate experimental design. However where does this matter - especially if the behaviour you want to explain is tangential to the exact evidence accumulation model.
