## My Thoughts

An interesting comparison of the two main frameworks for evidence accumulation models, the relative evidence methods (typical of models like the [[diffusion decision model|drift diffusion model]]) and absolute evidence methods with competing accumulators (like the [[Linear Ballistic Accumulator]]). However note they only consider an absolute evidence accumulator that uses a random walk process for accumulation.

[[Ami Eidels]] point in Journal Club was that given the level of model mimicry between these two classes of sequential sampling models, why should we care. I agree with several other [[Journal Club]] members that expressed the opinion that context matters for this question. If you are interested in the comparison between groups on a subset of parameters, such as drift rate differences between group A and B then the choice of model may be unimportant for your findings. If however you are a neuroscientist investigating the mechanism by which these models are/could be instantiated in the brain then the choice of which model is the best representation matter significantly more.
