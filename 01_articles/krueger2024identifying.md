## My Thoughts

An interesting variation on methods to select between possible [[decision strategies]] in data. They actually use a [[markov decision process]] for each strategy and a **meta-MDP** to choose the best decision strategy from those modelled.

Now the approach used has some roots in the [[Elementary Information Processes]] methods of [[Eric J. Johnson]] and [[John W. Payne]], however rather than treat each operation separately (READ, MULTIPLY, SUM) each mouse click in the [[Mouselab]] paradigm was given a specific cost that was used as the operational cost for that action.

The use of MDP is a nice addition here, and it is worth a mention in the [[Architecture of MultiAttribute Choice]] paper, however I am a little disappointyed in the Mouselab/process tracing approach used.

I do like the focus on *resource-rational* heuristics, but as with a lot of these process tracing studies the arena of decision proess that gets the most focus is on the information search process.
