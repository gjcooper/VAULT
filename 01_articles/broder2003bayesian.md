## My Thoughts

An early piece on selecting between competing decision strategies by using a [[comparative model fitting]] approach, which fits multiple models of decision strategies to data, and selects between them using the bayes factor for each model.

They argue that this offers superior application in particular to the choice between different [[decision strategies]]. In other words options like [[process tracing]] are more applicable to the [[information search]] part of the decision process. Also alternatives such as traditional [[outcome-based]] methods often have little contact between theory and data.

In general I am aligned with their approach. I may have missed it but I am not sure if the methods they have used work only on an individual level (I believe so) or if they work on aggregate measures. The stimuli they have used are typically binary state predictors (of which they used four). They do nod to extensions where there are more states per attribute - even continuous levels.

There is nothing here about response times - which is something I could argue my work on the [[Architecture of MultiAttribute Choice]] advances.
