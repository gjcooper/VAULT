## My Thoughts

An interesting marriage of the [[Take the Best]] heuristic and the [[Weighted Additive Rule|rational]] decision making model. Here they extend previous efforts to create a model combining these two prominent decision strategies, following on from [[lee2004evidence|Evidence accumulation in decision making: Unifying the “take the best” and the “rational” models]] they adapt the combined model to generalised models using a decision rule that allows for either more deterministic decision making or more probabilistic. They also add free parameters for cue validity to capture the inability for decision makers to know the exact cue validities.

One major contribution of this work is the incorporation of response time measures as an additional component towards selecting the correct model. However this is done using essentially a t-test (or Bayesian model selection) on a model that allows for differences between response times in two distinct decision types (hard vs easy via the rational model) vs one that treats these two decision types as the same under response time assumptions.

That is if the subject was using the [[Weighted Additive Rule|rational model of decision making]] then there should be different mean response times between the two decision categories. If there is no difference then the evidence is in favour of a generalised [[Take the Best]] decision strategy
