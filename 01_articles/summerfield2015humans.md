## My Thoughts

A discussion of the differences between perceptual (practised) decision making in people and non-human primates where the decision making is often "optimal" or close to it vs value based decision making where there are many notable decision biases in the literature.

Much here is neurobiologically focussed, talking about [[spike firing rates]], but in reality is a small primer on the [[efficient coding hypothesis]] as an explanation for the biases evident in value based decisions.
