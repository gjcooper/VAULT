## My Thoughts

An interesting intersection of categorisation (but not much theory here) and the perception of [[p-values]]. Although categorisation is mentioned there is no real exploration from a categorisation theory perspective, instead there is much ore emphasis on the [[mental number line]] effects as previously discovered in the literature.

These effects include the:
- [[mental number line#Size Effect|the size effect]]
- [[mental number line#Distance Effect|distance effect]]
- [[mental number line#Compatibility Effect|the compatibility effect]]

These effects are used as controls in the analysis for the study. The main part of categorisation theory that is used is the [[Categorisation#Categorical Perception Effect|categorical perception effect]].

They use relative rates of similar/different judgements and the response times of those judgement to argue that students with at least some statistical training do have the categorical perception effect for _p_-values around the traditional significant/non-significant boundary (_p_ = 0.05).
