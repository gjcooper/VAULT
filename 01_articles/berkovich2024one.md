## My Thoughts

A relatively small note as another point towards using [[hierarchical modelling|hierarchical bayesian models]]. The main premise here is that artificially fixing a parameter from a cognitive model to be the same across people is at best arbitrary. (In the case of fixing _sv_ for the [[Linear Ballistic Accumulator]]). Instead the authors argue you should use a hierarchical bayesian modelling framework and set a prior at the group level to be extremely narrow (basically fixing it). In this way the group level fixed prior creates a sort of "scale" for the parameter (as well as all other parameters), and now this scale is across all participants rather than within each participant.

#### How do they know this?

Two large scale simulation studies were run that simulated a large number of known parameters for a large number of trials. Comparison with standard fitting approaches were also done. In Study 1 all parameters except $v_{false}$ recovered well ((but if anything slightly less well than classical methods. In Study 2 the hierarchical approach worked significantly better, at least for the [[Pearson correlation]] (relative) - [[Intraclass Correlation Coefficient|ICC]] remained low.
