## My Thoughts

This thing is a tom and a half, but if you want some in depth discussion on the [[non-decision time]] parameter of [[evidence accumulation models]]. including both the [[diffusion decision model]] and the [[Linear Ballistic Accumulator]]. The argument is made that often the non-decision time used in these models is underspecified, and often varying with task parameters (like [[Speed Accuracy trade-off]]) that based on the cognitive interpretations of the parameter should **not** be influenced by the manipulation.

To counter this the authors propose a model that takes its impetus from neuronal recordings, most prominently from animal research and occulomotor responses. In this research there are distinct phases on the presentation of stimuli and response generation that relate to the beginnings of stimuli processingfinished
