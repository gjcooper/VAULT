## My Thoughts

A think piece on a [[Yale University]] project that is a component of the [[Individually Measured Phenotypes to Advance Computational Translation in Mental Health|IMPACT-MH]]. Some discussion of the merits and concerns with the proposed project - including the lofty goals but also issues such as:
- power (the proposed project does not target a specific psychiatric condition/treatment - and thus some subgroups may be very small)
- The spending of a large sum of money on longitudinal data for a series of tasks/models that are drawn primarily from the researchers expertise/bias. That is tasks developed that focus on [[reinforcement learning]] and [[schizophrenia]] may not be appropriate for all clinical subgroups.
- The researcher team focus leads to the possibility that anything learned through this project will not be widely adopted by other researchers. There is a tendency for researchers to prefer their own tools (models/tasks/etc) and this tendency could perhaps be alleviated by wider collaboration and consultation prior to the development of the project.
