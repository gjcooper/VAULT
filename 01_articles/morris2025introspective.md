## My Thoughts

The age old question of whether people have insight into their own decision making strategies has been long claimed - but not much emprirical evidence to support positions on either side of the debate. [[nisbett1977telling]] and some of [[J Edward Russo]]'s later work arguing people have little introspective access to their decision making, but others such as [[A G Abdul-Muhmin]] and [[B A Reilly]] arguing the opposite case.

Here [[Adam Morris]] and colleagues present the correspondence of self-reported decision strategies and attribute importance with the model-based assessment of attribute weights and heuristics used by the same participants.

They report that this correspondence is greater than predicted by a cohort of decision making scientists and experts, showing that our views of individuals insights into their decision making are misaligned at best.
